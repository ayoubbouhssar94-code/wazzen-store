# Brand Positioning, ICP, and Copy

## Brand

Arabic name: `وازن`  
English name: `WAZZEN`  
Meaning: balance, control, protection, comfort.

Position WAZZEN as a premium Saudi car comfort and protection brand engineered for Gulf heat, dust, family life, and daily driving.

## Positioning Statement

For Saudi drivers and families who care about protection, preparedness, and avoiding preventable car problems, WAZZEN provides practical solutions selected for Saudi heat and daily driving. Unlike cheap generic accessories, WAZZEN products are presented with clear mechanisms, honest limits, guarantees, and a local COD experience.

## Primary ICP

Primary buyer: Saudi women, 24-45, living in Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, Khobar, Makkah, Madinah, and major cities.

Likely situations:

- Drives children, family members, or herself daily.
- Cares about protecting the car and being prepared before a trip.
- Hates direct sun, low-pressure surprises, and small damage becoming expensive.
- Buys from Snapchat/TikTok when the product solves a real daily pain.
- Needs trust before ordering from a new brand.
- Prefers COD for a first purchase.

## Emotional Drivers

Speak to the emotion first, then prove:

- Relief: "أخيراً تركبين السيارة بدون حرارة تخنقك."
- Protection: "احمي ديكور سيارتك من شمس السعودية."
- Preparedness: "ضغط الإطار الصحيح معك قبل كل مشوار."
- Prevention: "عالج النقرة المناسبة قبل أن تتمدد."
- Control: "الشمس والضغط والضرر الصغير تحت السيطرة."
- Family care: "راحة لك ولأهلك من أول دقيقة."

## Language Rules

- Use Saudi-friendly Arabic, simple and premium.
- Avoid overly formal corporate Arabic.
- Use feminine phrasing often because the main ICP is women, but do not alienate male buyers.
- Use `الدفع عند الاستلام` everywhere near CTAs.
- Use trust phrases before asking for commitment.
- Never sound like a marketplace listing.

## Proof and Authority Rules

Allowed proof:

- Explain materials and mechanism.
- Mention heat/dust problem in Saudi climate.
- Use "inspired by thermal protection/material science" when talking generally.
- Use real review counts only when real data exists.
- Use placeholders clearly until real assets exist.

Do not claim:

- "SASO certified" unless certificate exists.
- "Clinically proven" because these are not medical products.
- Exact heat reduction percentages unless supported by product-specific test or cited content.
- "Made in Saudi" unless true.

Safe authority phrasing:

- `مصممة لظروف القيادة اليومية في السعودية`
- `مواد عاكسة للحرارة ومختارة للاستخدام المتكرر`
- `قياس رقمي وإيقاف تلقائي عند الضغط المحدد`
- `نظام حقن للنقر المناسبة مع حدود استخدام واضحة`
